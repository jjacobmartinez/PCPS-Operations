The Source of the Deficit: Mandated External Contracts

The entire budget shortfall is concentrated in 16 high-need students who cannot be accommodated by internal staff due to complex IEPs, single-rider needs, or extreme routing (e.g., out-of-county hotels).

The analysis clearly shows that highly specialized, single-student routes (like the $60,800/year contract) create extreme, unavoidable costs that are the root cause of the budget gap.

Identified Gap & Improvement Strategy

The primary gap is not routing inefficiency, but a critical lack of personnel to cover legally required, high-complexity routes. The department has initiated a proactive review to mitigate costs.

Phase 1: Compliance Audit

Re-evaluate all external routes against student IEPs and behavioral plans to confirm service levels are required and not over-accommodated.

↓

Phase 2: Feasibility Study

Analyze internal staff, vehicles, and shifts to identify any external routes that can be absorbed, even with overtime, for a net cost saving.

↓

Ideal Resolution

Acquire one dedicated driver and vehicle for McKinney-Vento routes, potentially saving ~$68,000 annually from external contracts.
